# Acme GTM ICP

Source: Acme GTM revenue team.

## Bottom line

Mid-market logistics and supply-chain software vendors with proprietary
shipment, route, or warehouse data, enough freight or fleet customers to feel
margin pressure, and either no AI roadmap or a thin AI feature that does not yet
change how operators plan loads, routes, or inventory.

## Hard gates

- Product company selling logistics, supply-chain, or freight software.
- B2B with carrier, shipper, broker, 3PL, or warehouse customers.
- Owns operational data such as shipments, routes, loads, ETAs, dock schedules,
  inventory positions, or freight invoices.
- Roughly 50-1500 employees, or smaller when clearly funded.
- Not AI-native as the founding category.

## Scoring settings

- AI gap: 30 points.
- Logistics data moat: 25 points.
- Operational urgency: 20 points.
- Budget/access: 15 points.
- Feasibility: 10 points.
- Tier A threshold: 75.
- Tier B threshold: 60.
- Reject or nurture below 60.

## Priority verticals

Priority verticals: freight TMS, last-mile delivery, fleet telematics, warehouse
management, supply-chain visibility, customs and trade compliance, cold-chain
monitoring, yard management.

## Limited AI traction checks

Look for missing AI route-optimization docs, paid AI SKUs, ETA-prediction case
studies, adoption proof, and operator-facing automation depth.
